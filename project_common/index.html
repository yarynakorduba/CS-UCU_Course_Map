Map of courses CS.UCU

semester 1

  • Discrete mathematics

    6 credits

    Teacher: Yuriy Shcherbyna

    The course of "Discrete Mathematics" has a wide range of purposes. Students should learn a significant amount of mathematical information and the ways of using it. The main reason is that fundamental concepts, facts and algorithms that are studied in this course are also used in "Programming Fundamentals ", " Probability theory "," Fundamentals of Artificial Intelligence "," Data Analysis "," System Modelling" courses. The main aim of this course is strict teaching of main theoretical concepts and its proofs, determination of algorithms designed for solving discrete problems, analysis and explanation of implementation for these algorithms.

  • Programming fundamentals

    4 credits

    Teachers: Andriy Romaniuk and Oles Dobosevych

    The course of "Programming Fundamentals" is basic discipline for "Computer Science" course. The aim of this course is to let students try out and develop their skills in Python programming language, to help them to get used to writing code in a clear manner, to get them acquainted with processes of solution designing and modern programming methodologies.

  • Calculus

    4 credits

    Teacher: Stepan Fedynyak

    The course of "Calculus" provides a proper development of basic mathematical skills for students, and gets them used to applying them. The main aim of this course is to develop a strict, rigorous and methodological approach to problems, to teach students the main methods of research and solution for mathematical problems, to develop independent distribution of knowledge and skills in approaching mathematical analysis tasks.

  • Learning and critical thinking

    3 credits

    Teachers: Ludmyla Kryvoruchka and Iryna Starovoyt

    "Learning and critical thinking" - is the unique course, which requires a good amount of courage to use your own mind. The aim of this course is to encourage investigation and thinking, to develop an actual critical thinking, which requires removal of own prejudice, and doubts in fundamental basics, to form individual criticism ethics, which will require proper introduction to the subject for further critical investigation.
